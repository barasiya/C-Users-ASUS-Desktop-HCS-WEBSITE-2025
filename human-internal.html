Introduction

The fields of liver transplant and hepato-pancreato-biliary (HPB) surgery play a crucial role in treating serious conditions of the liver, pancreas, and biliary system. Liver transplant involves replacing a diseased liver with a healthy one from a donor, while HPB surgery addresses conditions like pancreatic cancer, liver cancer, and bile duct diseases.

Liver Transplant

What is Liver Transplant?

Liver transplantation is a life-saving procedure for patients suffering from end-stage liver disease or acute liver failure. The procedure involves replacing the diseased liver with a healthy liver from a donor.

Indications for Liver Transplant

  • Cirrhosis of the liver
  • Liver cancer (Hepatocellular carcinoma)
  • Acute liver failure
  • Chronic hepatitis

Procedure and Recovery

The transplant surgery is typically performed under general anesthesia. Post-surgery, patients must take immunosuppressive medications to prevent organ rejection and undergo regular checkups.

Hepato-Pancreato-Biliary Surgery

What is HPB Surgery?

Hepato-pancreato-biliary surgery is a specialized branch of surgery that focuses on treating diseases of the liver, pancreas, and bile ducts. This may include procedures for liver tumors, pancreatic cancer, bile duct cancer, and other disorders.

Common HPB Surgeries

  • Liver resection (removal of part of the liver)
  • Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple procedure)
  • Bile duct resection and reconstruction

Challenges and Risks

HPB surgeries are complex, often involving multi-disciplinary teams due to the delicate nature of the organs involved. These procedures carry risks, including infection, bleeding, and organ dysfunction.
